# Systems To Explore

This is a short map of visual systems that are spiritually close to Clifford attractors, noise fields, grids, and evolutionary image making.

## Attractors And Maps

- Clifford attractor: compact, elegant strange attractor using sine and cosine recurrence.
- Peter de Jong attractor: another four-parameter trigonometric attractor with wispy, folded forms.
- Hopalong attractor: simple recurrence with crisp branching structures.
- Hénon map: classic discrete chaotic system; often looks sparse and architectural.
- Ikeda map: optical-cavity-inspired map with curling, galaxy-like shapes.
- Gumowski-Mira map: rich folded structures with a small number of parameters.
- Tinkerbell map: chaotic 2D map with butterfly-like forms.
- Lorenz system: continuous 3D chaotic flow; can be projected into 2D or rendered as trails.

## Geometry And Grids

- Truchet tiles and Wang tiles.
- Recursive subdivision.
- Voronoi and Delaunay diagrams.
- Penrose and quasiperiodic tilings.
- L-systems for plant-like or calligraphic branching.
- Circle packing and Apollonian gaskets.

## Noise And Fields

- Perlin and simplex noise.
- Curl noise flow fields.
- Domain warping.
- Fractional Brownian motion.
- Reaction-diffusion textures.
- Particle advection through vector fields.

## Search And Evolution

- Genetic algorithms for evolving parameters, palettes, shapes, or image filters.
- Interactive evolutionary art where a human picks favorite offspring.
- Hill-climbing and simulated annealing for composition search.
- Quality-diversity approaches that keep a gallery of visually different successes.

## Image-Based Work

- Dithering and error diffusion.
- Pixel sorting.
- Feedback and recursive transforms.
- Masked compositing.
- Palette extraction and recoloring.
